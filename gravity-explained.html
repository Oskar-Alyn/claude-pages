How it works · Gravity

Everything pulls on everything

Thousands of specks. Each one tugs on all the others. Watch where they end up.

Before the rule

Each dot is a speck of mass

Every dot up there is a tiny speck of mass. It pulls on every other speck, and every other speck pulls back. The brighter a dot, the faster it's moving.

Nothing is steering. No speck is in charge of the rest. Each one only feels the pull of the others and falls toward them.

That pull is the whole rule. Everything you're about to see comes from it.

The rule

Closer pulls harder

Two specks far apart barely feel each other. Bring them close and the pull grows fast. It's the same tug either way — distance is what decides how strong it gets.

Turn the pull up and the specks rush together. Turn it down and they barely gather.

A faint pull, they drift in loose loops
A strong pull, they snap into tight knots

Why it holds together

Every speck needs room

Get two specks close enough and the pull becomes enormous — enough to fling one clear off the screen. So each speck keeps a little space around it that the pull can't cross. A near-miss turns into a smooth swing-by instead of a slingshot.

No room, near-misses fling specks away
Room to spare, they swing past and stay

Letting it settle

A gentle brake

Left alone, the specks keep trading pulls forever and never sit still. A light brake bleeds off a little speed each moment. Slowly the churn calms, and the cloud settles into one flat, spinning disk — the shape of a galaxy.

No brake, it never stops churning
A light brake, it settles into a disk

The setting behind it all

How crowded is space?

One speck has nothing to pull on. A handful trace lonely loops around each other. But add enough and their pulls start to add up — small clumps gather, clumps pull in more, and the whole screen winds itself into spinning galaxies.

Put it together

Nobody placed the galaxies

None of these shapes are drawn in anywhere. They fall out of one rule, repeated between every pair of specks, many times a second. Pull, fall, settle.

A map that draws itself

No walker can see the map. Each just heads for the most-worn ground nearby and wears a little path of its own.

Before the rule

Each speck is one walker

Every glowing point up there is a single walker. It can't see the network it's part of. It only senses how worn the ground is in the patch right in front of it.

So no walker steers the map. Each one just reacts to the ground around it, following one rule.

The one rule

Follow a path, leave a path

A walker looks at the ground right around it, heads toward whichever patch is most worn, then presses a fresh bit of path down as it goes. With nothing worn yet, walkers just wander and trample the ground flat. Once paths start to show, they fall in behind each other and trails sharpen out of the open ground.

Just wandering, no paths form
Follows a path, trails appear

What falls out

The trail is a shared map

A walker can't tell its own path from anyone else's. It just heads for the most-worn ground, so every walker is reading the same map, the one they're all wearing in at once. The map doesn't live in any one walker. It lives in the ground they share.

It's the dirt shortcut worn across a grassy lawn: the more feet take a path, the clearer it gets, and the clearer it gets, the more feet it pulls in. Popular paths get more popular, and that loop wears a few wandering tracks into a network. It only comes alive in a crowd.

A handful, faint scribbles
A crowd, veins emerge

The other half

Unused paths fade

Treading is only half of it. The ground also slowly recovers: every path softens a little each moment. If paths never faded, every step anyone ever took would stay etched forever, and the whole ground would smear into one flat blur. Because paths heal, only the busy, well-walked routes stay clear. Forgetting is what keeps the map sharp.

Barely heals, it all smears
Heals steadily, crisp paths

One rule, a whole network

Every vein up there comes from the same loop: each walker heads for worn ground and wears it a little further, and millions of those small steps build the network you're watching.

A softer Game of Life

One rule, run on a smooth field instead of a checkerboard, and creatures swim out of the noise.

Before the rule

A field of living cells

Picture the screen as a grid of cells, each one somewhere between empty and full. The color is just how alive a cell is: dark where the field is empty, bright where it is brimming. Nothing here is a creature yet, only a smooth field of numbers.

The classic Game of Life flips cells hard on or off. Lenia lets them sit anywhere in between, which is the whole trick. Paint some life in yourself and watch it sit there, waiting for a rule.

Every cell looks around a ring

To decide what happens next, each cell adds up how much life sits around it. Not its touching neighbors, but a soft ring held a short distance away, brightest at the middle of the ring and fading to nothing at the edges. That donut on the left is the exact shape of the look.

Reaching out to a ring instead of the cells next door is what gives Lenia its scale. Widen the ring and the creatures grow with it.

A sweet spot decides its fate

Now the cell has a number: how much company it sees in that ring. A single sweet spot turns that into life or death. See about the right amount and the cell grows. See too little or too much and it fades. That is the entire rule, run on every cell at once, over and over.

Both fields below start from the same scatter. They differ by one setting, where the sweet spot sits.

The surprise

And then it starts to swim

Here is the part nobody puts in by hand. Leave that one rule running and the blobs do not just sit and pulse. Some of them settle into a lopsided shape that pushes itself forward, healing the damage you do to it and gliding across the field like a single-celled animal. Cut one in half and each half can grow back whole.

These swimmers were never drawn. They are simply the only shapes the rule lets survive while moving, so the field finds them on its own.

All of it, in your hands

The same field, a hundred worlds

You have met the whole rule now: a soft ring that measures company, and a sweet spot that turns it into life or death. Two dials shape that sweet spot. Where it sits decides how much company cells want, and how fussy they are about it decides whether shapes come out crisp or melt together.

Drag across the map and watch the very same field reorganize. Push too far and life starves to black or floods into shapeless blobs. Hold the narrow band between them and creatures appear.

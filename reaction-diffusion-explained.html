Patterns out of chemistry

Two chemicals, one self-copying reaction, and a single trick that breaks it into spots and mazes.

Before the forces

A dish with two chemicals

Picture the screen as a shallow dish, filled edge to edge with chemical A. Drip a little chemical B into it and the two start to react. Nothing here is a creature or a particle, just chemistry spread across a grid.

The color is simply how much B is in each spot: dark where the dish is empty, bright where the reaction runs dense. Drip a few drops in yourself and watch them take hold.

Force 1 of 3

The reaction copies itself

Where B meets A, it turns that A into more B. So every bit of B helps make the next bit: a single speck doesn't sit still, it spreads outward into fresh medium. Left alone, B would copy its way across the whole dish.

Can't catch, the speck fades out
Self-feeding, it spreads on its own

Force 2 of 3

So why doesn't it fill the screen?

Two things hold it back. A is constantly fed back into the dish, so the medium never fully runs out, and B is constantly killed off, so it can't just pile up. Tip that balance and the reaction either floods everything or starves. Leave it on the knife's edge and it settles into a living, lasting front.

Too little killing, B floods in
Balanced, it holds its shape

Force 3 of 3

Why a pattern, and not a blob?

One ingredient left, and it's what turns a spreading stain into structure. Both chemicals spread, but at different speeds: A drifts fast and B oozes slowly. Where they spread at the same speed, the edges blur into a flat wash. Where A outruns B, the front can't stay smooth, so it keeps breaking into spots and stripes.

Same speed, it dissolves to a wash
A outruns B, structure appears

All three at once

The same dish, a hundred worlds

You've now met all three forces: a reaction that copies itself, a feed-and-kill that holds it in check, and two speeds of spreading that fracture it into shape. Every spot and corridor below comes from those three running at every point on the grid at once.

And with patterns finally possible, the feed and kill rates together decide which one. Nudge them a hair and spots become worms, worms become a maze, a maze fills solid. Drag across the map and watch the very same dish reorganize.

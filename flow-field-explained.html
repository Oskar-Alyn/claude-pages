How it works · Flow Field

Riding an invisible current

No particle decides where to go. Each one just follows the arrow it's standing on.

Before the rule

Each streak is one particle

Every line of color up there is a single particle, drifting on its own. It can't see the flock, the screen, or where it's headed. It only reads the one arrow beneath it and takes a step that way.

Its color comes from which way it's flowing, so the palette ends up painting the shape of the motion itself. The silky streams aren't drawn anywhere — they're just thousands of these tiny steps, lined up.

The one idea

The screen is a hidden map of arrows

Behind every particle is an invisible vector field: at every point on the screen, an arrow saying which way to go. The arrows are woven from smooth noise, so nearby ones mostly agree and the flow bends in long, continuous curves.

A particle never plans. It looks up the arrow under its feet, nudges toward it, and moves. That's the whole simulation — everything below is just shaping this map, or the trails it leaves behind. Here's the field made visible:

Faint arrows: the field. Bright streaks: particles following it.

Shaping the map · 1

How tightly the map curls

Stretch the noise out and the arrows turn slowly across the screen, so particles ride broad, lazy sweeps. Compress it and the arrows spin through many small turns, breaking the flow into tight little whirlpools. Same idea, two very different worlds.

Stretched out, broad sweeping currents
Tightly curled, many small eddies

Shaping the streams · 2

How sharply particles turn

The same map can be drawn in different handwriting. When particles snap instantly to each new arrow, their paths come out jagged and nervous. When they turn lazily, easing toward the arrow instead of obeying it at once, the same field draws long, silky curves.

Snaps to the arrow, jagged paths
Turns lazily, long smooth curves

Shaping the map · 3

A map that breathes

The arrows don't have to hold still. Freeze the map and every particle traces the same fixed channels forever. Let it churn slowly and those channels drift, so the streams keep wandering into new shapes and never quite settle.

Frozen map, fixed channels
Churning map, the flow roams

Shaping the streams · 4

The streams are fading paint

A particle is really just a moving dot. The streaks are the trail it leaves, fading toward the background a little each frame. Wipe the frame clean every step and you see bare dots; let the paint linger and short steps stretch into long ribbons.

No lingering, just moving dots
Paint lingers, long ribbons

Put it together

Nobody drew these streams

None of the rivers and fountains you've watched are written down anywhere. They fall out of one rule — follow the arrow beneath you — repeated by thousands of particles at once.

See it for yourself. The particles are born from an emitter; switch its shape to change where every stream begins. And drag across the flow to tug the current around as it runs.
